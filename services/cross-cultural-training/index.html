Cross-cultural training

Orientation for working and living in Hungary

This training is designed for international professionals and organisations who experience friction, misunderstandings, or operational slowdowns after relocation – without being able to clearly identify the cause.

Rather than focusing on surface-level cultural tips, the training provides orientation: a way to understand what is happening beneath everyday interactions, and how to respond to it with greater confidence and precision.

How the training is built

From orientation to confidence

The training is not structured as a fixed set of modules. It follows an orientation arc that helps participants gradually move from uncertainty to clarity:

orientation → interpretation → practical situations

Participants are introduced to relevant cultural and historical context only where it directly supports understanding real-life situations. Background knowledge is always connected to lived experience – workplace interactions, social settings, and everyday moments where expectations and reactions do not align.

The emphasis is not on theoretical learning, but on recognising patterns in situations that participants already encounter, often without having the tools to interpret them clearly.

What this training is not

Clear boundaries

  • not a rulebook
  • not a list of do’s and don’ts
  • not a behavioural checklist
  • not a political or ideological narrative
  • not a touristic introduction to Hungary

By avoiding prescriptive formulas, the training ensures that what participants learn remains applicable across contexts, rather than tied to rigid scripts or specific scenarios.

Where this leads

From understanding to connection

  • more confident presence in everyday and professional situations
  • fewer misunderstandings and unnecessary escalations
  • clearer communication and expectation management
  • stronger, more functional working relationships

The result is not conformity, but agency: the ability to navigate Hungarian cultural contexts with understanding, proportion, and confidence.

This foundation can be applied both in individual relocation contexts and in organisational settings where international teams operate within Hungarian cultural environments.

Formats

  • Individual sessions for relocating professionals
  • Group trainings for international teams
  • Tailored corporate programmes in cooperation with HR and relocation partners

Formats are adapted to the specific situation rather than delivered as standardised packages.
