Services

I provide strategy-focused advisory support for senior leaders and organisations facing complex, high-stakes decisions. The work is not about delivering predefined solutions, but about creating clarity, structure, and decision readiness in situations where direction is unclear or contested.

Executive Strategy Advisory

Support for leaders navigating strategic uncertainty, competing priorities, or ambiguous organisational contexts. This includes reframing strategic questions, identifying constraints and leverage points, and supporting sound decision-making under complexity.

Typical situations include organisational change, role transitions, strategy realignment, and cross-functional tension at senior levels.

Decision-Making Support

Advisory work focused on how decisions are formed, framed, and enacted. The emphasis is on improving decision quality rather than accelerating decision speed.

This work helps leaders understand what is truly decidable in a given situation, what remains structurally uncertain, and how their position within the system shapes available paths forward.

Leadership and Organisational Perspective

Support at the intersection of individual leadership roles and organisational structures. The focus is on how roles, expectations, and information flows interact in practice - not only on formal org charts.

This service is particularly relevant where leadership challenges cannot be separated from organisational design, culture, or systemic constraints.

How This Work Is Typically Delivered

  • Confidential one-to-one advisory conversations
  • Structured strategic reflection and sense-making
  • Short- or medium-term advisory engagements
  • Close collaboration with HR or internal stakeholders when appropriate

What This Is Not

This is not management consultancy in the traditional sense, not a coaching programme, and not a delivery or implementation service. The advisory work remains focused on thinking, framing, and decision support.

If you are exploring whether this type of advisory support is relevant for a specific situation, an initial exploratory conversation is usually the best starting point.
